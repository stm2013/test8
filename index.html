Overview

The scanning tunneling microscope was invented by Gerd Binning and Heinrich Rohrer in 1981 in IBM, for which they received the Nobel Prize in physics for in 1986. The tool is a powerful microscopic technique capable of achieving nanometer resolution of surfaces.

Read more »

Vibrations

In scanning tunneling microscopy the choice of material is highly important. Floor vibrations with amplitudes up to 100 nanometers must be suppressed in order to allow an atomic resolution of 0.1 nanometers. This puts severe limits on the materials that can be used.

Read more »

Electronics

High demands are put on the electronics if atomic resolution is to be established. A reliable feedback controller and a high resolution actuating system is of high importance in order to scan the sample with good accuracy.
